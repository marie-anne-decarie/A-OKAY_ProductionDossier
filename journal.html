Sept. 23 - Initial film Proposal✧˖°.

// Working title: A-OKAY

// Synopsis: In a dark digital void, a young protagonist must face off with a malevolent entity taking the form of an eerie, corrupted clone of herself. The clash is restless, brutal, and highly destructive, and although our young hero is definitely up to the task, one is left to wonder if anyone can really come out unscathed of such a fight against oneself…

// Treatment: For this film’s technique, I am choosing 2D digital animation and digital compositing (After Effects) in order to best embody the cyberpunk genre and aesthetic I have in mind. The film will be incredibly fast paced, with bold perspective and high energy action. The movement of the animation will feel fast and brutal, translating the level of intensity of the fight happening on screen.

The visuals will consist of dark, minimalist backgrounds starkly contrasted with brightly colored, almost glowing characters. Imagery throughout the film will evoke computers and digital technology, code, glitches, and the cyberspace. Each of the opponents will have a slightly different visual treatment as a way to differentiate them more easily on screen. Mars, the main protagonist, will be fully colored and her movement will be a lot more fluid and expressive than her digital counterpart. The latter will appear as a simple glowing outline on the screen, and her movement will be a lot jerkier – perhaps her framerate will be lower. She will also have some unique animations when she gets hit by Mars, such as glitches and morphing in and out of her clone form. In general, the color palette will be rather limited, primarily green, blue and orange. I also don’t want the film to feel repetitive or static at any point, so I will experiment with bold transitions and effects such as flashes or shots in which the characters are simply a solid-colored silhouette against the background.

// What progress have I made on the film so far?

// Research

I have been watching and studying various material in order to learn how to properly create a fight scene. I started gathering reference videos into a YouTube playlist, including tips for writing/staging action scenes, actual fight scenes from live action and animated media, real self-defense video tutorials, etc. I sat down for a few hours and storyboarded some of the interesting fight moves, as well as took notes on what really makes a good movie fight.

The thing that stood out to me is the importance of establishing stakes: what do the characters have to lose if they don't win the fight? What are they fighting for? This leads me to...

// Writing

I sat down and started really thinking about the lore of my film. Who is Mars within her universe? Who is the clone? What makes the clone threatening, what is she trying to take from Mars? I wrote 3-4 pages of backstory which I won't be showing in the film just to really put myself in the world and mindset of these characters. The clone is trying to steal Mars' life away from her, to replace her. Mars has to fight to remain real, to remain alive; to avoid being replaced by a corporate, digital copy of herself. Themes of the film began to emerge as I was writing: the interconnected fear of technology and oneself; reality and the virtual; overcoming fear; AI and humans' terminal main character syndrom.

// Storyboarding and animatic

Okay, the development that I'm making in my head and in my personal notebook is cool and all, but where are we going concretely?

I had to make a first animatic for the entire film. I was really intimidated to start, but I found a process that worked for me and I just went for it.

Fighting is fast, restless, and so had to be my animating. I didn't want to spend too much time lingering on perfect blocking and tiny little details in this first pass of animatic: I had to go for movement, for intuition, for flow.

I did not fully storyboard the film, but rather wrote down the main beats and animated by using references and moves I had gathered during my research phase.

Last week, we got to swap our material with another artist in class and give feedback and suggestions. It was a very refreshing exercise and I got some really interesting notes.
